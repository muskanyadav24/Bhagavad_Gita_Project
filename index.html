Essence of Divine Knowledge

The Bhagavad Gita is a sacred dialogue between Lord Krishna and Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. As Arjuna hesitates to fight against his own kin, Lord Krishna imparts divine wisdom about duty, life, and the eternal soul. He explains that the soul is immortal—it neither takes birth nor dies. Every human being must perform their duties selflessly, without attachment to the fruits of actions. This is the path of Karma Yoga.

Krishna also reveals the paths of Jnana Yoga (knowledge), Bhakti Yoga (devotion), and Karma Yoga (action), emphasizing harmony between them. The Gita teaches that true liberation comes through devotion, discipline, and detachment. Ultimately, Arjuna overcomes his doubts and fulfills his duty. The Gita inspires humanity to act righteously, surrender to God, and live a life of purpose and peace.
